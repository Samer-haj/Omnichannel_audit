Section 01

Executive Summary

This report evaluates how Borrelli Crafters can improve its overall customer journey across different marketing channels in order to reach its goal of increasing awareness and achieving $2.5 million in annual revenue by 2027.

Borrelli Crafters already has a strong foundation. The company offers high-quality custom homes, is led by a Red Seal certified craftsman, and focuses on sustainability and long-term value. However, the issue is not the quality of the service. It is how that value is communicated and experienced across different customer touchpoints.

Identified Frictions

From the audit, three main problems were identified in the customer experience.

1

Low Visibility & Trust-Building

Weak trust-building at the awareness stage. The digital presence does not clearly explain the value of Red Seal certification, and there is limited educational content.

2

High Friction in Consideration

Customers feel unsure about pricing, timelines, and the building process. Because of this, they rely heavily on direct conversations instead of being guided through a clear digital system.

3

No Structured Post-Purchase Journey

Communication depends heavily on the founder, and there are no scalable systems in place for follow-ups, referrals, or long-term engagement.

Section 02

The Repositioning Strategy

To address these issues, this report recommends repositioning Borrelli Crafters as a trust-focused, process-driven brand supported by a stronger omnichannel strategy.

Strategic Implementation

  • Improving Visibility: Focusing on SEO and educational content that highlights the tangible benefits of Red Seal craftsmanship.
  • Reducing Uncertainty: Developing clearer processes, planning guides, and pricing frameworks that answer client questions proactively.
  • Building Relationships: Implementing better communication platforms and formalized referral systems to maintain post-purchase momentum.
If these changes are implemented, Borrelli Crafters can improve lead generation, increase conversions, and build stronger relationships with clients, ultimately supporting its revenue goal while solidifying its status as a premium brand.

Business Overview

The purpose of this audit is to evaluate how effectively Borrelli Crafters currently delivers a consistent and integrated customer experience across its marketing and communication channels. This report focuses on identifying gaps in the customer journey, understanding where potential clients experience friction, and recommending improvements that can help the business attract, convert, and retain high-value customers more effectively.

Organizational Structure

Borrelli Crafters operates with a lean, founder-led structure. The company is led by Matt Borrelli, who serves as both founder and lead craftsman. While this allows for strong quality control, it also means that client communication, sales, and marketing are heavily dependent on the founder.

The Technology Gap

Currently, Borrelli Crafters does not use any formal marketing technology tools. There is no CRM system in place to track leads or manage customer relationships, and there are no automation tools. Most processes are handled manually, which limits efficiency and scalability.

Section 04

Competitive Landscape

Borrelli Crafters operates in a highly competitive market. Key competitors include Gilbert + Burke, Timberline Custom Homes, R. Moore Homes, and Bosiak Construction.

These companies represent strong regional players that target similar high-value customers and position themselves around quality, experience, and trust. Across these competitors, several common marketing channels are used to attract and communicate with customers.

Competitive Advantage Observed Industry Best Practices
Process Clarity Successful competitors make their building process clear and easy to understand, reducing uncertainty for potential clients.
Proof of Work They provide strong visual and written proof of their work through case studies and project storytelling.
Thought Leadership They use educational content to position themselves as experts rather than just service providers.
The main takeaway is that competitors are effective not only because of what they build, but because of how well they communicate their expertise. Borrelli Crafters must better showcase its unique advantage: its Red Seal certified craftsmanship.

Market Segmentation

Market segmentation involves dividing a broad market into smaller, more homogeneous groups based on shared characteristics. In industries such as custom homebuilding, segmentation is particularly critical because purchasing decisions are high-involvement, high-risk, and infrequent.

Borrelli Crafters targets two primary market segments: "Wealthy and Wise" and "Big Fish, Small Pond." These can be strategically unified into a single, high-value audience defined as Legacy-Driven Landowners.

Demographics & Geography

The Profile

  • Age 38 to 68, HHI $200,000 to $500,000+
  • Net Worth $1M+
  • Executives, business owners, senior professionals
  • Seeking rural and exurban Ontario
  • Purchasing large properties (forested, waterfront)
Psychographics & Behaviour

The Mindset

  • Quality-focused, not trend-driven
  • Risk-averse but highly value-oriented
  • Strong appreciation for certified trades
  • Long research and consideration cycles
  • Heavy reliance on referrals and reputation

These customers view homebuilding not as a transactional purchase, but as a strategic, long-term investment, often tied to family legacy, lifestyle quality, and asset preservation.

Target Persona Profile

Richard & Elizabeth

Richard and Elizabeth are affluent, late-career professionals in their late 50s with a household income of approximately $325,000. They own a large, forested property in the Marmora region and are seeking to build a high-quality custom home that reflects long-term value, durability, and lifestyle goals. Their decision-making is highly deliberate, driven by a strong emphasis on working with certified professionals to reduce risk and ensure build quality.

Core Motivations

  • Creating a multi-generational legacy home
  • Protecting their financial investment
  • Partnering with a builder who demonstrates credibility, transparency, and craftsmanship

Brand & Service Affinities

  • Drawn to heritage brands and formal credentials
  • Prefer quiet luxury over flashy trends
  • Expect premium service delivery, clear communication, and professionalism
They are less sensitive to price and more focused on trust, process clarity, and long-term outcomes. They rely heavily on referrals and prefer educational, consultative interactions over traditional sales approaches.

Section 07

Customer Segmentation Strategy

Given the characteristics of this segment, Borrelli should adopt a Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) approach that prioritizes long-term relationships over one-time transactions.

Primary Targets

Priority Growth Segments

Advocates

Highly satisfied customers who actively refer others and have strong trust in craftsmanship.

Loyalists

Repeat clients who demonstrate ongoing engagement with the brand for secondary projects.

Potential

First-time clients exhibiting high potential for future projects and relationship building.

Lower Priority Segments

While Borrelli should prioritize high-value customers, it is equally important to identify segments that do not align with its premium positioning.

Transactional Clients

Primarily price-driven and focused on minimizing upfront costs rather than evaluating long-term value. Borrelli should selectively accept only profitable projects from this segment.

At-Risk Clients

Clients who show reduced engagement or satisfaction. Borrelli should implement targeted re-engagement strategies for high-value clients to rebuild trust.

Borrelli Crafters’ growth depends on prioritizing high-value, trust-driven customers while minimizing reliance on price-sensitive segments. Selecting the right customers is as important as acquiring them.

Journey Map: Acquisition

The following details the customer journey for Richard and Elizabeth, mapping their pain points, touchpoints, internal ownership, and critical gaps across the first five stages.

01

Awareness

Internal Ownership: Marketing Strategist

Pain Points & Desires: Wary of "cowboy contractors" who provide low-quality work. Desires a builder with national credentials (Red Seal) to protect his investment.

Identified Gap

Borrelli’s website lacks educational content explaining the value of Red Seal certification.

02

Interest

Internal Ownership: Marketing Manager

Pain Points & Desires: Frustrated by portfolios that only show "finished" aesthetics and desires to see the "bones" of the house.

Identified Gap

The communication regarding the value of certifications is currently unclear. Most content focuses on the final end result rather than the hidden quality of the build.

03

Engagement

Internal Ownership: Sales Lead

Pain Points & Desires: Difficulty verifying the authenticity of craftsmanship claims. Desires to see concrete proof of craftsmanship.

Identified Gap

There is a significant lack of detailed case studies that go beyond final photos to explain the technical execution and craftsmanship involved.

04

Consideration

Internal Ownership: Founder

Pain Points & Desires: Strong price anxiety due to unclear project scope. Desires an early, rough price estimate.

Identified Gap

There is limited educational or process-oriented content available to ease the persona's technical concerns. Missed opportunity to showcase Matt's approachable personality.

05

Conversion

Internal Ownership: Sales Lead

Pain Points & Desires: Fears "leaky bucket" pricing and project risks. Desires cost transparency and reliability.

Identified Gap

Over-reliance on manual effort. Lacks visualization technology to justify the premium.

Mapping the operational build phase through to long-term advocacy.

Section 10

Future State & Recommendations

To support growth, the future customer journey must create a trust-building experience at every stage. This transforms confusion into confidence.

Future Enhancements

  • Awareness: SEO improvements and educational content establishes authority and increases discoverability.
  • Interest: Website case studies, project galleries, and testimonial videos demonstrate craftsmanship.
  • Consideration: Downloadable planning guides and pricing frameworks reduce uncertainty.
  • Post-Purchase: Digital client portal with progress updates, timelines, and communication tools improves transparency.
  • Advocacy: Referral programs and follow-up campaigns convert satisfied clients into brand advocates.
Action Plan

5 Recommendations

1. Implement a CRM system To manage leads and automate follow-up processes.
2. Content strategy Create materials that educate prospects on the value of Red Seal certification.
3. Standardize the sales process Introduce clear pricing frameworks and planning guides.
4. Communication platform Provide consistent updates, bridging the technical phase communication gap.
5. Referral program Formalize post-purchase systems to capture advocacy.
