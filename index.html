Section 01

Executive Summary

This report evaluates how Borrelli Crafters can improve its overall customer journey across different marketing channels in order to reach its goal of increasing awareness and achieving $2.5 million in annual revenue by 2027.

Borrelli Crafters already has a strong foundation. The company offers high quality custom homes, is led by a Red Seal certified craftsman, and focuses on sustainability and long term value. However, the issue is not the quality of the service, it is how that value is communicated and experienced across different customer touchpoints.

Identified Frictions

1

Low Visibility

Weak trust building at the awareness stage. The digital presence does not clearly explain the value of Red Seal certification, and there is limited educational content to help potential clients understand why Borrelli is different.

2

High Friction in Consideration

Customers feel unsure about pricing, timelines, and the building process. Because of this, they rely heavily on referrals or direct conversations instead of being guided through a clear system.

3

No Structured Post Purchase Experience

Communication depends heavily on the founder, and there are no systems in place for follow ups, referrals, or long term engagement.

Strategic Shift

The Repositioning Strategy

This report recommends repositioning Borrelli Crafters as a trust focused, process driven brand supported by a stronger omnichannel strategy. This includes improving visibility through SEO and educational content, reducing uncertainty with clearer processes and pricing guidance, and building long term relationships through better communication and referral systems.

By aligning its digital presence, sales process, and client experience, the company can position itself not just as a skilled builder, but as a leading premium brand.

Section 02

Business Overview

The purpose of this audit is to assess how well Borrelli Crafters currently delivers a consistent customer experience across its marketing and communication channels, identifying gaps and opportunities for growth.

Objectives & Goals

Targeting $2.5M by 2027

Borrelli Crafters' overall business goal is to increase awareness in the Marmora and surrounding area and grow revenue to $2.5 million annually. To support this goal, several SMART marketing objectives are proposed.

50%Web Traffic Increase
3%Target Conversion
30Qualified Leads Monthly
20%Referral Leads

Organizational Structure

Borrelli Crafters operates with a lean, founder led structure. The company is led by Matt Borrelli, who serves as both founder and lead craftsman. While this allows for strong quality control, it also means that client communication, sales, and marketing are heavily dependent on the founder.

The Technology Gap

Currently, Borrelli Crafters does not use any formal marketing technology tools. There is no CRM system in place to track leads or manage customer relationships, and there are no automation tools. Most processes are handled manually, which limits efficiency and scalability.

Section 03

Competitive Landscape

Borrelli Crafters operates in a highly competitive and fragmented market, where many builders rely on reputation, referrals, and local presence to attract clients. Key competitors include Gilbert + Burke, Timberline Custom Homes, R. Moore Homes, and Bosiak Construction.

These companies represent strong regional players that target similar high value customers and position themselves around quality, experience, and trust.

Competitors are effective not only because of what they build, but because of how well they communicate their expertise. Borrelli Crafters must better showcase its unique advantage: its Red Seal certified craftsmanship.

Observed Industry Best Practices

Process ClaritySuccessful competitors make their building process clear and easy to understand, which helps reduce uncertainty for potential clients.
Proof of WorkThey provide strong visual and written proof of their work through detailed case studies and project storytelling across their websites and social channels.
Thought LeadershipThey use educational content, blogs, and guides to answer common customer questions, positioning themselves as experts rather than just service providers.
Section 04

Market Segmentation

In custom homebuilding, purchasing decisions are high involvement, high risk, and infrequent. Borrelli Crafters targets a unified, high value audience defined as Legacy Driven Landowners.

Demographics & Geography

The Profile

  • Age 38 to 68, Household Income $200k to $500k+
  • Net Worth $1M+
  • Executives, business owners, senior professionals
  • Seeking rural and exurban Ontario (small towns, cottage regions)
  • Purchasing large properties (forested, waterfront)
Psychographics & Behaviour

The Mindset

  • Quality focused, not trend driven
  • Risk averse but highly value oriented
  • Strong appreciation for certified trades (Red Seal)
  • Long research and consideration cycles
  • Heavy reliance on referrals and reputation
Target Persona Target

Richard & Elizabeth

Richard and Elizabeth are affluent, late career professionals in their late 50s with a household income of approximately $325,000. They own a large, forested property in the Marmora region and are seeking to build a high quality custom home that reflects long term value, durability, and lifestyle goals. Their decision making is highly deliberate, driven by a strong emphasis on working with certified professionals to reduce risk and ensure build quality.

Section 05

Customer Segmentation Strategy

Given the characteristics of this segment, Borrelli should adopt a Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) approach that prioritizes long term relationships over one time transactions.

Primary Targets

Priority Growth Segments

Advocates
Highest Value

Highly satisfied customers who actively refer others and have strong trust in craftsmanship.

Loyalists
High Value

Repeat clients who demonstrate ongoing engagement with the brand for secondary projects.

Potential
Growth Target

First time clients exhibiting high potential for future projects and relationship building.

Lower Priority Segments

While Borrelli should prioritize high value customers, it is equally important to identify segments that do not align with its premium positioning.

Transactional Clients

Primarily price driven and focused on minimizing upfront costs rather than evaluating long term value. They compare contractors based on price and exhibit low brand loyalty. Borrelli should selectively accept only profitable projects from this segment and avoid targeting them in its marketing strategy.

At Risk Clients

Clients who show reduced engagement or satisfaction. They communicate less frequently and provide limited referrals. Borrelli should implement targeted re engagement strategies for high value clients to rebuild trust.

Section 07

Future State & Recommendations

To support growth, the future customer journey must create a trust building experience at every stage. This transforms confusion into confidence.

Future Enhancements

  • Awareness: SEO improvements and educational content establishes authority and increases discoverability.
  • Interest: Website case studies, project galleries, and testimonial videos demonstrate craftsmanship.
  • Consideration: Downloadable planning guides and pricing frameworks reduce uncertainty.
  • Post Purchase: Digital client portal with progress updates, timelines, and communication tools improves transparency.
  • Advocacy: Referral programs and follow up campaigns convert satisfied clients into brand advocates.
Action Plan

5 Recommendations

1. Implement a CRM system

Manage leads and automate follow up processes.

2. Content marketing strategy

Create materials that educate prospects on Red Seal value.

3. Standardize the sales process

Introduce clear pricing frameworks and planning guides.

4. Digital communication platform

Provide consistent updates, bridging the technical phase gap.

5. Referral program

Formalize post purchase systems to capture advocacy.

Borrelli Crafters already has the most important asset: a reputation for craftsmanship and quality. By strengthening its omnichannel strategy, the company can transform this expertise into a scalable growth engine.
