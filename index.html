1. Executive Summary

This report evaluates how Borrelli Crafters can improve its overall customer journey across different marketing channels in order to reach its goal of increasing awareness and achieving $2.5 million in annual revenue by 2027.

Borrelli Crafters already has a strong foundation. The company offers high-quality custom homes, is led by a Red Seal–certified craftsman, and focuses on sustainability and long-term value. However, the issue is not the quality of the service—it is how that value is communicated and experienced across different customer touchpoints.

Three Main Problems Identified

1. Low Visibility & Weak Trust

The website and digital presence do not clearly explain the value of Red Seal certification, with limited educational content at the awareness stage.

2. High Friction

Customers feel unsure about pricing, timelines, and the building process during consideration, relying entirely on direct founder conversations instead of guided systems.

3. No Post-Purchase Structure

Communication depends heavily on the founder. There are no systems in place for follow-ups, structured referrals, or long-term engagement.

 {/* 2. Repositioning Strategy */}

2. The Strategic Shift

"To address these issues, this report recommends repositioning Borrelli Crafters as a trust-focused, process-driven brand supported by a stronger omnichannel strategy."

The findings show that Borrelli Crafters does not need to change its core service, but rather strengthen how that service is communicated and delivered across channels. By aligning its digital presence, sales process, and client experience, the company can create a more seamless and trustworthy journey for potential clients.

4. Org & Competitive Landscape

Organizational Structure & Martech

Borrelli Crafters operates with a lean, founder-led structure led by Matt Borrelli, supported by one lead foreman and one lead carpenter. While this allows for strong quality control, client communication, sales, and marketing are heavily dependent on the founder.

Currently, Borrelli Crafters does not use any formal marketing technology (martech). There is no CRM system in place, making lead tracking manual. The lack of automation limits efficiency, causing the customer experience to vary based on Matt's availability rather than a consistent system. To support omnichannel growth, adopting basic martech tools is critical.

Competitive Landscape

Borrelli Crafters operates in a highly competitive and fragmented market. Key regional competitors include Gilbert + Burke, Timberline Custom Homes, R. Moore Homes, and Bosiak Construction.

"Successful competitors make their building process clear to reduce uncertainty, provide strong visual proof via case studies, and use educational content to position themselves as experts."

The main takeaway is that competitors are effective not only because of what they build, but because of how well they communicate their expertise. Borrelli Crafters has a massive opportunity to outshine them by actively showcasing its unique advantage: Red Seal certified craftsmanship.

{/* 5. Market Segmentation Overview */}

5. Market Segmentation Overview

Market segmentation involves dividing a broad market into smaller groups based on shared characteristics. In custom homebuilding, segmentation is critical because purchasing decisions are high-involvement, high-risk, and infrequent. Unlike fast-moving consumer goods, custom homebuilding requires significant financial investment, long decision timelines, and absolute trust.

Effective segmentation must go beyond basic demographics and focus on deeper behavioural and psychographic drivers, such as risk tolerance, trust expectations, and long-term value orientation.

Unifying the Audience

Borrelli Crafters targets segments like "Wealthy and Wise" and "Big Fish, Small Pond." We have unified them into a single, high-value audience defined as:

"Legacy-Driven Landowners"

Affluent rural or exurban homeowners with high income, strong asset ownership, and a preference for spacious properties. They view homebuilding not as a transactional purchase, but as a strategic, long-term legacy investment tied to family, lifestyle quality, and asset preservation.

This unified approach is highly effective because both older and younger affluent buyers demonstrate similar behaviours: they conduct extensive research, rely heavily on referrals and credibility signals, and prioritize working with certified professionals to minimize construction risk.

7. Segmentation Strategy (CLV)

Given the high-investment nature of the market, Borrelli Crafters should adopt a Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) approach that prioritizes long-term relationships and referrals over one-time transactions. Growth depends on prioritizing trust-driven customers while selectively managing price-sensitive segments.

Priority Segments (High Value)

Advocates (Highest Value)

Highly satisfied customers who actively refer others and maintain strong trust in the craftsmanship. They are the primary engine for word-of-mouth growth.

Loyalists (High Value)

Repeat clients who maintain ongoing engagement for renovations, upgrades, or secondary builds.

Potential Loyalists (Growth)

Recently completed first-time clients who have a high potential for future projects if the relationship is nurtured.

Lower Priority Segments

Transactional Clients (Low CLV)

Primarily price-driven customers focused on minimizing upfront costs rather than evaluating long-term value. They exhibit low brand loyalty.

Strategic Implication: Selectively accept only highly profitable projects; do not target in marketing.

At-Risk Clients

Clients showing reduced engagement or neutral feedback post-interaction, posing a risk to reputation if poorly managed.

Strategic Implication: Implement targeted re-engagement strategies to rebuild trust.

10. Future State Customer Journey

To support growth, the future customer journey must create a trust-building experience at every stage. This shifts reliance away from manual founder interactions toward scalable, automated digital systems.

Awareness

SEO improvements and educational content should directly answer key questions: What does Red Seal mean? How much does it cost? This establishes immediate authority.

Interest

The website transforms into a visual proof engine, utilizing detailed case studies and testimonial videos that validate craftsmanship rather than just end aesthetics.

Consideration

Downloadable planning guides and clear pricing frameworks are introduced to explain the construction process, directly reducing prospect uncertainty and friction.

Conversion

Implementing CRM systems and structured consultation booking tools will allow the company to systematically track leads and improve follow-up closing rates.

Post-Purchase

Introducing a digital client portal provides real-time progress updates, timelines, and communication tools, eliminating the client's fear of 'communication blackouts'.

Advocacy

Structured referral programs, testimonial capture systems, and proactive CRM follow-up campaigns will convert satisfied clients into active brand advocates.

 {/* 11. Recommendations & Conclusion */}

11. Strategic Recommendations

To successfully implement the future-state strategy and achieve the goal of $2.5M in revenue, five major foundational changes are recommended.

1

Implement a CRM System

Adopt technology to manage leads centrally, automate follow-ups, and transition sales tracking away from manual founder effort.

2

Develop Structured Content Marketing

Create an SEO strategy based on educational content that explicitly defines the value of Red Seal certification and answers early buyer questions.

3

Standardize the Sales Process

Deploy downloadable guides and structured pricing frameworks to reduce friction and build trust during the consideration stage.

4

Introduce a Digital Client Platform

Utilize platforms like Buildertrend to formalize construction updates, drastically improving transparency and client peace of mind.

5

Create a Referral & Testimonial Program

Systematize the capture of social proof upon project completion to actively fuel word-of-mouth rather than waiting passively.

Conclusion

Borrelli Crafters already has the most important asset: a genuine reputation for exceptional craftsmanship and quality. By executing these omnichannel marketing recommendations—improving discoverability, reducing decision friction, and formalizing post-purchase engagement—the company can transform its expertise into a scalable growth engine that secures its position as a premium legacy builder.
